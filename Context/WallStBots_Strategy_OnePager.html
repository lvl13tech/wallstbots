BOT13

Precision Intraday Momentum

BOT13 hunts the market's strongest single-day movers and only acts when conditions clearly favor a winning trade. Its core rule: be in positions or be in cash — never be losing without a reason. Three times each trading day (open, midday, close) it reassesses the universe, logs every decision, and either deploys capital or stands aside.

Weekly · Every Monday

ORACLE

Adaptive Weekly Momentum

Every Monday morning ORACLE scores the entire universe on four dimensions of momentum and quality, then builds a concentrated portfolio of the five highest-conviction names. Positions are held for the week — no intraday noise, no emotional overrides. The model only enters names showing both recent and intermediate-term strength.

Monthly · 1st Trading Day

WIZARD

Quality Long-Horizon Momentum

WIZARD thinks in months, not minutes. On the first trading day of each month it builds a deliberate 8-name portfolio anchored on risk-adjusted returns and trend quality. It rewards names that have moved well over 20 and 60 days, have strong Sharpe-proxy scores, and are trading above their 50-day moving average. Sizing is quartile-tiered so the strongest picks carry the most weight.

Passive Baselines — The Benchmarks to Beat
Passive · Equal Weight

EQUALIZER

The Pure Market Proxy

EQUALIZER allocates $1,000 to every stock in the universe — no selection, no timing, no judgment. It represents what a purely diversified, opinion-free investor would earn. If the active bots can't beat EQUALIZER consistently, the strategies need work. It's the harshest baseline because it removes all skill assumptions.

  • Exactly $1,000 per position across all 55 stocks — zero discretion
  • Never rebalances — buy and hold the initial positions
  • Benchmark question: does active stock-picking add value over pure diversification?
💡 EQUALIZER wins when the entire market rises uniformly. Active bots should outperform it in trending or volatile markets where selection matters.
Passive · Cap Weighted

TITAN

The Institutional Proxy

TITAN mirrors how large institutional portfolios are constructed: half the capital is concentrated in the top 10 highest-conviction "heavyweight" names ($2,750 each), and the remaining half is spread equally across the rest of the universe. It asks the question: does our active selection beat a simple size-tilted strategy?

  • 50% of capital in top 10 heavyweights at $2,750 each
  • Remaining 50% split equally across all remaining names
  • Static from inception — no rebalancing, no tactical moves
  • Benchmark question: does precision selection beat a cap-weighted tilt?
💡 TITAN wins when large-cap leaders dominate the market. Active bots should outperform it when smaller or overlooked names lead the rally.
